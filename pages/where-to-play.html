For Players in the United States

If you live in the US

US residents have the most options. Retail purchases are available everywhere Powerball is sold; online play depends on your state.

Retail (everywhere)

Buy at any participating retailer in 45 states plus DC, Puerto Rico, and the US Virgin Islands. Gas stations, supermarkets, and convenience stores generally sell tickets. Easiest option, but you have to be present.

State iLottery apps

Six states currently sell Powerball directly through official apps: Pennsylvania, New Hampshire, Virginia, Michigan, Georgia, and North Dakota, plus the District of Columbia. Check your state lottery's website.

Courier services (40+ states)

Services like Jackpocket buy tickets on your behalf inside your state. Available in over 40 states. You pick numbers in the app, they scan the actual ticket, you cash out winnings without leaving home.

Subscriptions

Many state lotteries let you sign up for automatic entries — never miss a draw, no need to remember. Best when a long jackpot run is building.

For Players in the United Kingdom & Ireland

If you live in the UK or Ireland

UK and Irish players can't buy a real Powerball ticket without flying to the US, but they have two solid options: licensed couriers that buy tickets stateside on your behalf, or licensed betting platforms that pay out the same prizes.

Ticket couriers

e.g. TheLotter

  • Buy an actual physical ticket in the US
  • You own the ticket — if your numbers win, you collect the real prize
  • Service fee added to ticket price (typically $3–$5)
  • For very large jackpots, you may need to travel to the US to claim

  • Often cheaper than couriers

For Players Elsewhere

If you live anywhere else

From Canada and Australia to the Philippines and South Africa, dozens of countries have access to Powerball through licensed ticket couriers. The general rule: pick a service licensed in a regulated jurisdiction (Malta, UK, EU, Curaçao).

Whether you go with TheLotter, LottoKings, or another courier, look for these markers of legitimacy: a verifiable license from a recognized regulator, a real physical office address, third-party security audits, and a track record of paying out winners. Avoid any service that's vague about how tickets are bought or where prizes are paid from.
