Frequently Asked Questions

When are Powerball drawings held?

Powerball drawings are held three times a week — Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday at 10:59 PM Eastern Time. The drawing takes place at the Florida Lottery draw studio in Tallahassee.

Where can I play Powerball online in the US?

A handful of US states offer official online Powerball sales, including Pennsylvania, New Hampshire, Virginia, Michigan, Georgia, and North Dakota, plus the District of Columbia. In other states, courier services like Jackpocket buy tickets on your behalf inside your state.

Can I play US Powerball from the UK or Europe?

Yes. Licensed lottery couriers such as TheLotter and LottoKings purchase official US Powerball tickets on your behalf at retail outlets inside the United States. You receive a scanned copy of the actual ticket as proof of purchase.

What is the biggest Powerball jackpot ever won?

The world-record Powerball jackpot of $2.04 billion was won on November 7, 2022, by a single ticket sold in California. The runner-up is $1.817 billion, won on December 24, 2025, in Arkansas.

What is the cash value of a Powerball jackpot?

The advertised jackpot is an annuity paid over 30 graduated payments across 29 years. Winners can instead choose a one-time lump-sum cash payment, which is roughly 45% to 55% of the advertised total depending on interest rates.

Are Powerball winnings taxable?

Yes. The IRS withholds 24% federal tax upfront on prizes over $5,000, and you'll owe additional federal income tax depending on your bracket. Most states also impose income tax on lottery winnings — though a few states (including Florida, Texas, and Washington) do not.

How do I claim a Powerball prize?

Prizes under $600 can usually be claimed at any participating lottery retailer. Larger prizes must be claimed at your state lottery office, with required forms and identification. Jackpot winners typically have 60 days to choose between the annuity and cash option.
